Intro

Oahu, known as "The Gathering Place," is the most populous and dynamic of the Hawaiian Islands. The culture is a melting pot of native Hawaiians and immigrants ranging from Japanese, Filipino, Chinese and Korean.
This unique culture and blend of ethnicities gave birth to local food found only in Hawaii like Spam Musubi, Malassada, Ahi Poke, Garlic Shrimp and Loco Moco. If you get a chance, you should definitely try some of the local dishes out.
There's a lot of great places to go. If I had to pick, here are my top 3 recommendations!

  • 1. Ala Moana Center

  • 2. Haleiwa Joes

  • 3. Lanikai Beach

Ala Moana & Waikiki

  • Ala Moana Center

  • Shopping

  • Map

Definitely gotta check Ala Moana out which is the world's largest open air shopping center. Theres 350 plus shops and restaurants to explore, and hopefully you can find some good deals.
If you have a JCB credit card, you can catch a free Waikiki Trolley pink line ride from Ala Moana to Waikiki.

  • Hy's Steak House

  • Steak

  • Map

One of the most popular high end steak houses. It's pricy but if you are looking for something fancy and want to eat at one of Oahu's best steakhouses, this would be the spot.

  • Yard House

  • American

  • Map

If you are looking for a good american restaurant with great beer to go with it, I recommend checking out yardhouse. They have happy hour if you can make it before 5:30pm.
Their menu choices are all tasty but I recall their nachos, onion rings, and chicken wings being really good.

  • House Without A Key

  • American

  • Map

Good music and a fantastic beach view. I recommend to get a reservation around 5pm so you can get a good table to watch the sunset.
It's pricy but their Mai Tai is famous so I think it's worth it to try one while enjoying the Hawaii vibes.

  • Steak Shack

  • Steak

  • Map

Great steak that's on the cheaper side. Good to take on the go for lunch, especially to a nearby beach.

  • Musubi Cafe IYASUME

  • Spam Musubi

  • Map

If you want to try a famous Hawaii local dish called Spam Musubi, you should go here. This store is now a chain but their "Honten" started here.
They also have regular onigiris if you are looking for something more traditional.

Diamond Head

  • Ono Seafood

  • Ahi Poke

  • Map

You have to try ahi poke if you can in Hawaii. It's also relatively cheap and good to go for lunch. I recommend getting their spicy ahi and shoyu ahi.

  • da Cove Health Bar and Cafe

  • Acai Bowl

  • Map

Best tasting Acai Bowl in Honolulu in my opinion. Perfect afternoon snack to quench your thirst and sweet tooth after a long day at the beach.

Hawaii Kai

  • Costco Hawaii Kai

  • Shopping

  • Map

If you have Japanese Costco card you should be able to get inside.
It's better to visit if you're in Hawaii Kai area with a car since you can grab a quick meal and some gifts also on the way back after the beach.
I don't know why but Costco foodcourt tastes better after the beach and I recommend to try the Chicken Bake and Sundae if you get a chance to go.

  • Koko Head Crater Trail

  • Hiking Trail

  • Map

This is one of the most challenging hikes on the island. It's worth it to go if you're prepared to have a tough cardio workout and get rewarded with the best view of the Hawaii Kai area.
Don't take kids here!!

  • Makapu'u Beach Park

  • Beach

  • Map

One of the best beaches on Oahu. Highly recommend checking it out if you're in Hawaii Kai area and get a chance to go.

Kailua

  • Lanikai Beach

  • Beach

  • Map

Considered to be one of the most beautiful beaches on Oahu, so much so that it's nicknamed Heavenly Beach.
The water is usually super calm so you can have a great time enjoying the sun and swimming near the beach.
There is no designated parking area so you will have to find open street parking, should be easier to find if you go on the weekdays.

  • Pillbox Trail

  • Hiking Trail

  • Map

You can get a fantastic view of the Lanikai beach area below. Definitely one of the fun hikes I've experienced. I recommend going on this hike in the morning and then going to Lanikai beach to cool off after the hike.

Kaneohe

  • Haleiwa Joe's

  • Hawaiian

  • Map

They have good seafood and steak options, they try to source locally and the scenery is amazing here.
If you go, you have to try get the prime rib for sure and they don't take reservations so try to go earlier to open time which is from 4pm.

  • Kualoa Ranch

  • Outdoor Activity

  • Map

Amazing mountain views, this location is also used as a filming spot for many famous shows and movies like Jurassic Park.
They have multiple outdoor activities and tours to choose from such as Horseback riding and UTV tours. Check out their tours and make a reservation in advance from their website if you're interested.

North Shore

  • Waimea Bay

  • Beach

  • Map

Waimea Bay is a must-visit if you're in the North Shore especially in the Summer. You will see a drastic change depending on the season.
During the winter time, the waves are huge. Summer time is much calmer with no waves and the big rock near the beach is a popular jumping spot.

  • Matsumoto Shave Ice

  • Shave Ice

  • Map

One of the most famous shave ice shops on the island. If you're in the North Shore and looking for a cool treat, check this local shop out.

  • Shrimp Trucks

  • Garlic Shrimp

  • Map

North Shore is famous for it's surfing spots but also the shrimp trucks. There's a lot of trucks in the area that offer tasty Garlic Shrimp so I suggest one that's close by, or you can try multiple tastes at different trucks.
Giovanni's Shrimp Truck is the most well known and considered to be the original shrimp truck.
